Cyber Security in Healthcare

  • NOV 19 2021

Adoption of healthcare technology is complex, and it takes time to design and implement. Because healthcare organizations have not kept up with new trends and dangers, they are susceptible. Many healthcare systems have been forced to embrace digitization across all department levels. Adopting new technologies and new ways of working to satisfy demand has led to hospitals overlooking cyber security, thereby becoming vulnerable to cyber threats.

Healthcare cyber security is becoming increasingly significant. Hacking and IT security attacks have progressively increased over the years, and many healthcare organizations have battled to defend their network perimeter and keep cybercriminals at bay. Here are the ways to improve cyber security in healthcare systems:


Staff Training

Security awareness training should be given to everyone involved in the healthcare system, including doctors, staff, volunteers, and vendors. Using real-life hacking and phishing instances is the best technique. Some companies intentionally phish their team as a form of training. Employees need to be taught the procedure for reporting suspicious behavior for quick response.

Software Updates

Software developers offer updates for their programs and apps frequently for better performance and as a security measure. Such updates are essential because they safe computers from opportunistic cyber threats. Most of the successful system hacks result from exploiting known security flaws of an outdated program or app. Many hackers will look through freshly published lists of system vulnerabilities and then look for opportunities to exploit to access servers.

Use of Firewall

The usage of firewalls can considerably lessen the impact of cyber-attacks on healthcare. A firewall can be installed at various places throughout your network to safeguard assets on both sides by screening traffic and deleting data packets that contain risks. Remote employees can utilize firewalls to set up secure, encrypted virtual private networks (VPNs).

Passwords and Strong Authentication

Strong passwords are a great way to stay safe in an online world. A strong password is made up of long strings of letters, numbers, and characters and does not have any terms that can be found in a dictionary. Never use a set of numbers and letters that can be predicted, and do not repeat the same password for another account. In a multi-authentication system, a password should be the only step to ease a password shared or stolen by the incorrect person. Using a secondary form of identification, such as biometric data or a physical device that creates a temporary passcode, will improve your security.
