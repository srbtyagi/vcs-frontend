Clinical Study-1

Dynamics of patient satisfaction and patient revisits

Understanding the dynamics of patient satisfaction and patient revisits proved essential. Nurses are professionals who are in more contact with patients than other medical workers, which impacts patient satisfaction. Thus, finding a model that ensures medical services quality, patient satisfaction, and intent of visiting is necessary. The case study will reveal that efforts such as nurses showing kindness and consideration, technical and functional quality, and a proper physical environment improve the three factors.

The Problem

There existed a problem regarding medical service quality, patient satisfaction, and intent of patient visits when providing an appropriate link among the three could improve outcomes. Medical environments came out as the working environments where nurses bring out their best. One of the reasons why nurses are essential in medical settings is their skill set. Nurses can perform various roles, including leadership, medical administration, diagnosis, and discharge. In this regard, it became essential to find out how nurses can ensure that patients experience quality services, get satisfied, and have positive intentions on visits. The challenge came when medical environments had trouble balancing the three concepts. In many ways, medical service quality and patient satisfaction appear detached. However, they coexist, and that means finding a way that the two interact. Nurses can be essential parties to the process, although it is evident that they will need interprofessional collaboration. Take the case when nurses might not know the extent of interprofessional collaboration to ensure medical service quality. However, the case study provided answers such as pegging healthcare quality on “professional skills of the healthcare provider” (Woo, & Choi, 2021). Nurses can ensure professionalism since that will contribute toward patient satisfaction. The impact on improved intent of patient visits will then become possible.

The Intent of the Case Study

That said, the case study will expose essential parts that nurses can use to improve the wellbeing of patients. Patients came out as the primary reasons why nurses and other clinical workers work. Achieving patient needs became one of the essential goals of nursing. The roles extend to caring for caregivers. That said, patient needs include quality medical services, patient satisfaction, and intent of patient visits. The case study will offer a model that will harmonize efforts to ensure the three goals get achieved. Nurses should understand that patient satisfaction encourages patients to revisit the institution (Woo, & Choi, 2021). The satisfaction then emanates from quality services. As pointed in the roles of nurses above, nurses need to provide that they excel in their skills set in one way or another. The case study will show specific ways nurses and other medical professionals can solve the dilemma.

The Solutions

Kindness and Consideration

Kindness and consideration became an essential part that nurses can play to ensure quality medical services. Kindness and consideration varied in many ways. Woo, & Choi (2021) stated that kindness and consideration looked into the attitudes of medical workers and the consideration of staff. Nurses need to show a positive attitude when dealing with patients. Showing that they are kind and empathetic toward what the patient went through enhanced the quality of nursing services. Nurses should not risk appearing unkind, significantly when affected by compassion fatigue. Nurses become prone to appearing unfriendly in ER environments. In such cases, some psych assistance would help. Also, nurses need to become considerate in their work settings. Considerate nurses tend to understand some shortcomings that patients might have. The shortcoming includes them not accurately knowing their medical history, perhaps due to pain experienced at the time. By showing kindness and consideration, nurses lead to an improved medical services quality that enhances patient satisfaction. The model proposed also required the provision of quality work.

Quality Work

The effectiveness of any professional gets rooted in their professionalism and skill set. Nurses who receive adequate education and training can provide quality work deliveries depending on their deliveries. Nurses perform several relatively complex tasks such as removing catheters, caring for wounds, administering medication using syringes, and participating in diagnosis. The case study showed that medical personnel should present with technical and functional quality as perceived by the patient (Woo, & Choi, 2021). Patients who feel that their nurses attain the necessary quality will be satisfied with the services offered. The medical environment appeared sensitive, and patients and caregivers sought the best services. The sensitivity of a human being’s health should not be taken lightly. Nurses can add to their technical and functional quality by responding to patients’ questions and understanding the problem. Such factors appear important primarily in ICU and ER environments when caregivers panic.

Physical Environment

The physical environment where the nurses work held a lot of potential regarding improving patient satisfaction and enhancing their chances of revisiting. The physical environment ranged from cleanliness to the effectiveness of equipment. It is essential to note that these analyses happen from the perspective of the patient. When patients feel that the physical environment is clean and welcoming, they get even more satisfied with their services. The impact comes when the patients will resonate more with nursing services and perceive them as quality. The impact led to the intent of visit in case of issues later or if they get an appointment. Thus, nurses became essential parties to the process when they contribute to the behavioral nature of patients concerning receiving treatment.

Recommendations for Practice

That said, the case study showed that nurses could apply the three recommendations to ensure that patients receive appropriate quality and become intentional about revisits. Take the case when nurses should show kindness and consideration in their work environment. When patients feel that the nurse who served them presented with technical and functional quality, they will perceive the medical services as entailing quality. Thus, nurses should look into that. Third, nurses should ensure a proper physical environment by providing cleanliness and available working equipment to assure patient satisfaction and revisits.

Clinical Study-2

Reducing Patient Re-admission through Discharge Note

Communication cut across all industries, and it is one of the most excellent tools that make the operations effective and efficient. The same in nursing communication is very important to the client and for the healing processes. The patient would need continuous updates of what is happening at every step and every kind of transition. One of the most critical communication is the Discharge Note, which ensures that the patient's transition plays a critical role in the patient's recovery process. Hospital discharge is made as a vulnerable time in a patients care transition (Mackles, 2020). It makes an effective execution that implies the client's recovery trajectory. Nurses' most important tool in their toolbox is to enhance patients' healing process by delivering communication discharge instruments for the patients. Discharge notes can be either wrong or practical, depending on the approach. The article will use a case of discharge that is wrongly done and the one right done as a reference point.

A Scenario

The nurse gets into the wards where Mrs Lopez is admitted, and she greats hers and directly tells her, hey! Mrs Lopez, you're going home today. Nurse: How are you, Mrs Lopez? Mrs Lopez: I'm good. Nurse: You are going home today; your doctor called us and told us you can be released. He also gave a summary of medication that you can buy at your near home pharmacy. As well, you can call him for the follow up of the clinic arrangement. Can you call your husband to come for you? Mrs. Lopez: On shock, really? My husband is at work, and he doesn't get off often. Nurse: Without much listening, she handover the discharge note for Lopez to sign. From the scene that makes a lousy discharge approach. The nurse tries to assume that patient will be good enough and excited to go home, and very satisfied. The reporting is not done well, and the transition is poor because the primary doctor is not present and the nurse it for granted. The whole aspect creates a dis-ease and uncomfortable situation for Mrs Lopez because she has no idea how she will get home, no explanation of best care when she is at home after leaving the hospital security.

Risk at Discharge

Some studies have been done, especially one by Dr Alan Foster and the group that found that more than 20% of the patients whore given leave out from a given hospital sustained worse events within 2 weeks of being discharged from the hospital. The study revealed that more than 400 continuous patients’ discharged 76 had some worse condition after the hospital. Most of these conditions are preventable during the discharge by giving better instruction and a better transition process (Mackles, 2020). The medication associated situation was the most cause of the worse conditions.

Another study conducted by Jenk Williams and Coleman from the Medicare patients given the leave out from hospital showed that 19.5% of 11855 patients needed re-admission in 30 days. More than 34% went for readmission within 90 days. Readmission can have cost consequences which can be controlled when the proper discharge is done. From the study, $17.4 billion was being paid annually for the readmission. With such information, the hospital and nurses must optimize patients' discharges to improve their care.

Other issues which face the discharged patients and their care providers from the hospital are. In most cases, the results are likely to be lost in the wash and not subjected upon. The patients may also be scheduled to come for the more outpatient tests and diagnostic which they never show up even after phone calls and reminders.

An evaluation of more than 2644 patients in Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston showed that they had a pending laboratory and radiologic tests. The study showed that only 40% of the patient came back for the remaining tests, and 10% of those findings were potentially actionable (Ross, 2020). The physicians who have the pending results to give were also tested, and out of 105, 61%, which is 65 of the physicians, could not remember they had pending results to give the clients. The results indicated that out of the forgotten 65 results, 24 were actionable, and 8 needed fast attention.

So what makes good practice?

A successful discharge note is significant to the continued healing of the patient and the provision of post-discharge care. The discharge dialogue must be initiated by the clinician attending the patient and the nurse in charge of discharging the patient. The discharge must have good information that assures the patient a safe denature from the hospital and well-structured follow-up. It is imperative to initiate a family member or the caretaker or an engagement of a home health aide in the conversation (Ross, 2020). There are several things that the patient must understand, first the reason for the current situation, for example, surgical and things that transpired during the hospital period. Second, a patient must understand their medical condition and things to follow the outpatient. The patient must receive warnings and signs of adverse conditions which could come along. The patient must be given emergency contact that is available in 24 hours, the care provider's name after discharge bearing name, address, and phone number.

Good Discharge Note Scenario

Discharge Planner: Mrs Lopez sometimes at the hospital, things can be confusing when the discharge is being done, especially when the patient has some severe conditions, but I am here to make sure that you are okay with each plan that should be followed. Kindly tell me a brief review of what doctor you will be seeing and the date of appointment. Mrs Lopez; Yes, I got as scheduled with Dr Fernandez at 9.00 am on Tuesday. Discharge Planner: How will you get to the appointment? Mrs Lopez: My husband is available and will bring me in his car; alternatively, we have a home driver who will drive me. Discharge Planner: Any medicine that you will be taking home? How will reach the prescription be filled, and do you know the purpose of the medication? Mrs Lopez: The prescription has been handed over to me. The medicine is for a diuretic to control high BP. I will take a pill each day, and we can access it from 45th street.

Conclusion

Generally, to improve the patient care outcome and reduce the rate of readmission, the patient must be given a good guide and good transition. The discharge must cover all perspectives of recovery using the Teach-Back technique to deliver constructive dismissal with all information like the medication, schedules, diets and best practices. During this time, it is easier to use the Internet of Things, Health Apps to ensure that the patient gets the best discharge note and continues alerting of what to do for a full recovery. In case of low literacy, the planner can add pictures, videos, simulations of the discharge and use of a 3-model.
